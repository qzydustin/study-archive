Introduction

Westwood Studios is a video game development company in the United States. Founded by Brett W Sperry and Louis Castle in 1985. Acquired by Electronic Arts (EA) in 1998 and closed in 2003.

Founder

Brett Sperry is an American video game designer, a fine arts gallerist, and a professional photographer.

In 1985, Brett Sperry and business partner Louis Castle created Brelos Software from a garage in the suburbs of Las Vegas, Sperry. As president and executive producer. Two months later, the two changed the company name to Westwood Associates, and in 1992 again changed the name to Westwood Studios.
